Nanoa Tribe × NTU Plus Academy : Cooperation Project

An industry cooperation project which involves a local indigenous tribe and NTU plus academy. , and the aim of this project is to define their current difficulties and provide improvement strategies through interviews and visits.

Background of Nanoa

Nanoa Tribe is composed of Atayal People, an indigenous group based in Yilan, Taiwan. The tribe’s goal is to build and run a chicken farm big enough to support the whole tribe by making sufficient and stable profit through selling chicken and creating proper job vacancies for those in need in the tribe. The chicken they breed is Zhongxing Red Feather Native Chicken, developed from Taiwan native chicken through lab breeding by Zhongxing University.

Pain Points

After several visits and interviews with Limuy and Hayung, which is the couple that is the first to successfully run a chicken farm in Nanoa and is now monitor / recruiter of all farms, we concluded that there are two main pain points within their current status.

1/ The lack of resources, equipment and funds to build a better environment for chicken farming + The naturally high humidity adding to the difficulties
2/ The instability in sales sources, which relies on referrals between regular customers (mostly Nanoa villagers) and is lacking a channel to reach new customers. Prolonged sales periods can impact the timeline for raising a new batch of chickens (as the old chickens are still on the farm ).

We decided to work on the second pain point. If it works well, it could also help make bigger profit, further improve the resources in general.

Thoughts on Pain Point 2

Issue
1. Unstable sales sources relying on referrals between regular customers
2. Long sales duration because it requires waiting for orders to come in gradually, selling in batches over time. It takes a while to sell everything completely

Goal - Shortening the Duration
Reducing the sales duration can avoid spending excessive effort on marketing and allow for an earlier start in raising the next batch of chicks.

Solution
To achieve the goal, we decided to build a website to better introduce the farm, Nanoa, and how the chicken is special there.There is a simple form in this website, people easily fill it out to order chicken directly from the farmers. This will shorten the sales duration significantly and at the same time help build a more systematic management over their sales.

To expand the customer base, they could start by sharing this website on their facebook page or on other platforms where potential customers can see.
